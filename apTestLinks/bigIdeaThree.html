Big Idea #3

Key Ideas In Big Idea #3:

Big Idea Three covers chemical reactions, which involves either the transfer of electrons, change in state of matter, or rearrangement of atoms.

Important Things to Know:

  •       What oxidation numbers are, how to determine them, and what oxidation and what reduction are

  •       What a galvanic/voltaic cell is and how it is different from an electrolytic cell.

  •       What the anode and cathode are in a cell and what they do.

  •       What current, charge, voltage, amperage, coulombs, joules, and Faraday's constant are and how to use them in a variety of calculations.

  •       What cell notation is, how to read and write it, and how to use it to determine a given cell.

  •       What the standard potential of a cell is, how to calculate it, how to use it in calculations, and when the reaction is spontaneous.

  •       What the standard free energy of a reaction is, how to calculate it, how to use it in calculations, and when the reaction is spontaneous.

  •       How to determine the potential of a non-standard cell using the NERNST equation.
