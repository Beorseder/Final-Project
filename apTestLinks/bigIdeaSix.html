Big Idea #6

Key Ideas In Big Idea #6:

Big Idea Six covers any attractions between any atoms and molecules that can form or be broken.

Important Things to Know:

  •       What an equilibrium constant is, how to determine the expression for determining an equilibrium constant for any given reaction.

  •       How to calculate equilibrium constants using either concentrations of ions or partial pressures of gases and what the constant means about the reaction.

  •       How to determine either Kp or Kc using the formula Kp = Kc (RT)^Î”n .

  •       How the orientation of molecules during collision can result in a reaction occurring.

  •       What Le Chatlier's principle is and how it applies when adding or removing a substance from a system and changing the temperature or pressure of a system.

  •       What the driving force in a reaction is, what the reaction favoring either reactant or products means, and what shift in equilibrium is.

  •       How to use the Kc of a reaction, along with the starting concentration of reactants, in order to find pH or concentration of products at equilibrium and halfway to equilibrium.

  •       When determining the K of a reverse reaction, it is the reciprocal of the forward reaction.

  •       When using hess's law to determine the K of an overall reaction, it is the K's of the reactions that go into it multiplied by eachother.

  •       How to determine if the forward or reverse reaction will occur in order to reach an equilibrium given the concentrations of substances.

  •       How to approximate for the concentration of a substance when it is not known to determine the concentration of another using the 5% rule.

  •       How to determine the concentration of a substance using the quadratic formula.
