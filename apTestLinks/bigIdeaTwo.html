Big Idea #2

Key Ideas In Big Idea #2:

Big Idea Two covers anything that has to do with atomic bonding, Lewis dot structures, geometric shapes, and intermolecular forces.

Important Things to Know:

  •       What an ionic bond is, how to determine the lattic energy of an ionic bond, and the strength of an ionic bond compared to other types of bonds..

  •       What a covalent bond is, how the atoms in a covalent bond are arranged and how the electrons in between atoms are.

  •       How to determine the geometric shape of a molecule and how this shape affects its movements and intermolecular forces.

  •       What resonance structures are, how to draw them, and when to draw them.

  •       How electronegativity creates interatomic attractions and how these interatomic attractions can affect the overall movement of a molecule.

  •       What intermolecular forces are, how to determine what intermolecular forces are at play, the strengths of each, and their significance.

  •       What intermolecular forces are, how to determine what intermolecular forces are at play, the strengths of each, and their significance.
