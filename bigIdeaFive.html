Big Idea #5

Key Ideas In Big Idea #5:

Big Idea Five covers how the laws of thermodynamics can explain and predict chemical reactions and changes in state of matter.

Important Things to Know:

  •       What enthalpy is, what the tendancies for enthalpy are for the formation of elements, molecules, and ions, and what any given enthalpy means about that substance.

  •       How to determine the standard enthalpy of a reaction using the standard enthalpies of formation of atoms and molecules.

  •       What entropy is, what the tendancies for entropy are for various elements, molecules, and ions, and what any given entropy means about that substance.

  •       How to determine the standard entropy of a reaction using the standard entropies of atoms and molecules.

  •       What Gibb's Free Energy is, how to use it to determine spontaneity, and how to calculate it using enthalpy, entropy, and temperature of a reaction.

  •       How to rearrange the Gibb's Free Energy equation in order to solve for the temperature at which equilibrium occurs.

  •       What the Gibb's Free Energy Isothem equation is and how to calculate the concentration of a substance or equilibrium using it.

  •       What Faraday's constant is and how to derive the standard potential of a cell from Gibb's standard Free Energy.

  •       What a phase diagram is, how to read one, and what the significant of one is.

  •       What a coffee cup calorimeter and bomb calorimeter are and how to determine the enthalpy for the water and substance using specific heat capacities, mass of the substance, and change in temperature.
