Big Idea #4

Key Ideas In Big Idea #4:

Big Idea Four covers rates of chemical reactions and how to determine them.

Important Things to Know:

  •       What the definition of rate is and what the rate expression is for any given reactant or product.

  •       How to determine if a rate is affected by concentration, of the effect of it in such a situation.

  •       How temperature affects the rate constant and the consequent rate.

  •       How a catalyst can affect the rate and how surface area can affect the rate.

  •       What collision theory is and how orientation and energy of the colliding atoms/molecules affect them.

  •       What the relationship between change in time, change is entropy, and change in enthalpy is.

  •       What the order of a reaction is and what the significance of a reaction's order is.

  •       How to determine orders of individual reactants and the overall equation, as well as how knowing these individual orders can affect the rate of the reaction.

  •       How to determine the order of a reaction based off of different graphs.

  •       How to determine the amount of time that has passed in a zero, first, or second order reaction through initial and final concentrations, or to get concentrations.

  •       How to determine the rate constand and what the significance of its units are with a different order reaction.

  •       What a reactant is, what a product is, what a catalyst is, and what a reaction intermediate is.

  •       How to know if a mechanism is consistent / how to determine the slow step in a mechanism using the rate law.
